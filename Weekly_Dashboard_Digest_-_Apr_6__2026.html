Ten weeks in — 72% of Q1 elapsed — and the funnel is showing real mid-stage momentum while top-of-funnel and the bottom remain under pressure. Meeting Booked surged to 67% projected ($24.3M actual, 49% of target), up 27pp from Week 6, powered by a monster $6.1M week from AMER. SALs climbed sharply to 56% projected (113 actual, 41% of target). Pre-Pipeline more than doubled its projection from 14% to 24% ($7.9M actual). But MQLs slipped to 36% projected (728 of 2,770), and Qualified Pipeline remains critically behind at 16% projected ($1.9M of $15.7M). Events continue to dominate — $16.0M in attributed Meeting Booked ARR — but conversion through Discovery and Qualified remains the quarter's defining gap. With only 3 weeks left, the close-rate math is severe: Qualified needs $4.6M per week to hit target versus $240K last week.

What's working
Wins & highlights
Meeting Booked hit a quarter-high week at $6.1M
$6.1M in Week 9 · $24.3M cumulative
The biggest single week of the quarter by a wide margin — driven by AMER ($5.4M) and powered by Shoptalk Spring ($3.0M) and HIMSS ($3.5M in cumulative pipeline). Meeting Booked pacing jumped from 40% to 67% projected, closing a 27pp gap in four weeks.
SALs recovered sharply — 56% projected, up from 35%
113 SALs · 41% of target
After languishing at 35% projected in Week 6, sustained weekly acceptance (10–17 per week) brought SALs to 56% projected. The MQL-to-SAL handoff that was breaking earlier in the quarter has stabilized.
Pre-Pipeline nearly doubled — 24% projected, up from 14%
$7.9M actual · 77 Discovery opps
Pre-Pipeline ARR climbed from $2.4M to $7.9M since Week 6, with 14 new Discovery opps in Week 9 alone — the quarter's best week. Events ($3.9M) and Organic ($1.1M) are the primary engines, and EMEA contributed $1.35M — a strong regional signal.
AI pipeline ratio remains dominant at 11.5:1
$1.70M AI vs. $148K Comms Qualified
AI Qualified ARR has widened its dominance from 6.7:1 to 11.5:1. The market pull toward the AI offering continues to strengthen, representing 92% of all Qualified pipeline.
Branded search volume already exceeds Q1 target
5,369 impressions · 149% projected
Against a 5,000 quarterly target, branded search has already delivered 5,369 impressions with 3 weeks remaining. A strong signal that brand awareness campaigns are gaining organic traction.
Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Qualified Pipeline critically behind — $1.9M of $15.7M (16% projected)
$1.85M actual (12%) · Needs $4.6M/week for 3 remaining weeks
Only 15 opps have reached Qualified all quarter. Even with $7.9M in Pre-Pipeline, Discovery-to-Qualified conversion has been glacial. AMER Qualified at 23% projected is the strongest region but still far behind; APAC at 6% and EMEA at 13% are deeply off-track. At 16% projected, this metric is essentially irrecoverable at current velocity.
Enterprise Qualified still collapsing — 4% projected
$284K actual vs. $10.2M target
Enterprise represents 65% of the total Qualified target yet has generated only $284K — less than 3% actual. Mid-Market ($1.24M) continues to carry the load but cannot compensate for an Enterprise gap of this magnitude. This remains the single biggest risk to the quarter.
MQL pacing slipped to 36% projected — declining trajectory
728 of 2,770 · 26% actual
MQLs dropped from 39% projected to 36% — moving in the wrong direction. Week 9 delivered only 49 MQLs (vs. 77 in Week 4). Paid Search (158 total, 18 last week) remains the top MQL channel but weekly volumes are inconsistent. Events MQLs spiked to 63 QTD but delivered zero the week of March 21.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion deteriorated further to 8%
8% · ↓57% QoQ
Down from 18% in FY26 Q4 and 9% at the Week 6 read. The conversion efficiency problem compounds every gain made upstream — 728 MQLs are producing only 15 Qualified opps. The funnel is leaking at the critical mid-stage juncture.
APAC Qualified at 6% projected — no movement
$232K Qualified on a $5.2M target. Only 4 Qualified opps all quarter from the region. APAC Discovery ($1.4M) suggests pipeline exists but is not converting. With 3 weeks left, the gap is likely unrecoverable without a fundamental shift.
Inbound Paid Pre-Pipeline still severely underperforming at 2%
$171K in Pre-Pipeline Discovery ARR against a $9.6M channel target. Paid Search is the second-largest MQL channel (158) but pipeline conversion is nearly non-existent. ROI on paid spend remains deeply negative.
Social followers pacing at 27% — well short of target
796 followers gained against a 3,000 Q1 target. Weekly gains have been inconsistent (42, 51, 25, 16 in recent weeks). At current pace, projected attainment of ~1,100 will miss the goal by over 60%.

Key takeaway: Week 9 was a tale of surging volume at the Meeting Booked stage — the $6.1M and 63 new opps represent a massive influx, primarily from AMER events (Shoptalk Spring, HIMSS, Digital Travel Connect). But downstream, Pre-Pipeline Discovery fell 31% to $1.0M, and Qualified registered zero new pipeline for the first time since Week 3. MQLs were essentially flat at 49 — the third consecutive sub-50 week. SALs pulled back from 17 to 10 after two strong weeks. The critical question for the final three weeks: can any of this $24.3M in Meeting Booked pipeline accelerate through Discovery and into Qualified before quarter close?
