FY2027 Q1 is now effectively complete — 96% of the quarter elapsed — and every major pipeline metric finishes well behind target. Projections have locked in at the same levels reported last week, confirming the quarter's final shape. MQLs land at 910 of 2,770 (37% projected, 33% actual), with Week 12 contributing just 14 MQLs — the weakest week of the quarter and a steep drop from Week 11's 126-MQL surge. SALs finish at 154 of 277 (63% projected, 56% actual) with no new acceptances recorded in the final week. Meeting Booked ARR closes at $27.7M of $49.8M (63% projected), with Week 12 adding $208K across 4 opps — all from APAC. Pre-Pipeline Discovery holds at $12.3M of $44.9M (31% projected), and Qualified Pipeline finishes at $2.86M of $15.7M (21% projected) — the quarter's most significant miss at a $12.8M gap. Events carried the quarter with $17.9M in Meeting Booked ARR (78% of total) and $6.9M in Discovery (64% of total). The AI-to-Comms Qualified ratio stands at 6.5:1 ($2.48M AI vs. $378K Comms). With Q1 now closing, the entire organization must pivot to executing on the $12.3M Discovery pool that enters Q2 and addressing the structural gaps that produced a quarter of systemic underperformance.

What's working
Wins & highlights
Events delivered $18.7M in Meeting Booked ARR — 76% of all pipeline created
$18.7M Events · 76% of $24.5M total
Events was the undisputed pipeline engine of Q1. Across 40+ activations, Events generated $18.7M in Meeting Booked ARR, $6.9M in Discovery (64% of total), and $1.17M in Qualified pipeline. Shoptalk Spring ($3.9M), HIMSS ($3.6M), ViVE ($3.4M), eTail Palm Springs ($1.7M), and CCW Exchange UK ($1.3M) were the top five producers. Events Discovery reached 73% projected attainment against its $9.8M channel target — the only channel approaching its goal.
Branded search volume crushed target — 146% projected attainment
6,975 actual vs. 5,000 target · 7,299 projected
Branded search impressions finished Q1 at 6,975 — already 40% above the 5,000 quarterly target with projected attainment at 146%. This signals strong and growing brand awareness that should feed downstream organic and direct pipeline in Q2. Weekly impressions held steady in the 500–680 range throughout the quarter.
$12.3M Discovery pool provides the strongest Q2 entrance pipeline
120 Discovery opps · $12.3M ARR entering Q2
Despite the Q1 shortfall, the quarter produced 120 Discovery-stage opportunities totaling $12.3M in ARR — a substantial pool to convert in Q2. AMER leads with 68 opps ($8.0M), followed by APAC at 32 opps ($1.8M) and EMEA at 11 opps ($1.8M). Weeks 10–11 generated $4.3M in Discovery alone, meaning much of this pipeline is fresh and actionable.
EMEA Meeting Booked outperformed at 77% projected — strongest geo by %
77% projected · $3.8M of $5.0M target
EMEA was the top-performing region on a percentage basis, projecting 77% attainment with $3.8M in Meeting Booked ARR against a $5.0M target. The CCW Exchange UK ($1.3M) and HIMSS-related EMEA activations drove the majority. EMEA also generated $1.8M in Discovery pipeline, providing a strong foundation for Q2.
Marketing Programs sourced 60% of Qualified Pipeline
$1.70M from Marketing Programs · 25% of target
Marketing Programs was the most effective Qualified pipeline source at $1.70M (60% of total $2.86M Qualified), projecting 25% against its $7.8M target. While still below target, this significantly outperformed Sales Prospecting ($405K, 15%) and SDR Prospecting ($150K, 5%), confirming marketing-sourced pipeline as the primary revenue engine.
Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Qualified Pipeline finishes at 21% — $12.8M gap to target
$2.86M actual (18%) · $3.25M projected of $15.7M target
The quarter's most critical miss. Qualified Pipeline closed at $2.86M against a $15.7M target — an 82% shortfall. Only 24 opps reached Qualified stage across 13 weeks (fewer than 2 per week). Enterprise Qualified landed at $894K — just 10% of its $10.2M target. SDR Prospecting sourced only $150K of a $3.1M target (5%). The conversion bottleneck from Discovery ($12.3M) to Qualified ($2.86M) — a 23% conversion rate — is the fundamental structural issue that must be solved in Q2.
Pre-Pipeline Discovery finished at 31% — $32.5M below target
$12.3M actual (28%) · $14.1M projected of $44.9M target
Discovery pipeline reached $12.3M of a $44.9M target — a $32.5M gap. Outside of Events ($6.9M, 73% of channel target), every other channel severely underperformed. Direct generated just $640K against an $18.4M target (3%). Inbound Paid produced $480K of $9.6M (5%). Email/Marketing Automation hit $136K of $2.2M (6%). The over-reliance on Events for pipeline generation is a significant concentration risk.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion collapsed to 11% — down 41% QoQ
11% conversion · down from 18% in Q4
Despite generating 910 MQLs, only 24 reached Qualified stage — an 11% conversion rate, down 41% from Q4's 18%. The funnel is generating top-of-funnel volume but failing to convert. This points to potential lead quality degradation, sales follow-up velocity issues, or qualification criteria misalignment. Every 1pp improvement in this rate would have yielded approximately $1.2M in additional Qualified pipeline.
Week 12 was the quarter's weakest — all metrics collapsed
14 MQLs · $208K Mtg Booked · 4 opps · 0 SALs
The final full reporting week saw just 14 MQLs (down 89% from 126), $208K in Meeting Booked (down 82%), 4 meetings booked (down 78%), and zero Sales Accepted meetings. No new Discovery or Qualified pipeline was recorded. This confirms the quarter ended with a whimper, not momentum — making Q2 Week 1 acceleration even more critical.
APAC Qualified at 8% — $346K of $5.2M despite $3.1M in Meetings Booked
APAC generated $3.1M in Meeting Booked ARR and $1.8M in Discovery but only $346K reached Qualified (5 opps). The conversion rate from Discovery to Qualified is among the lowest in the funnel. The AICC event drove $502K in Meetings and $370K in Discovery, but downstream conversion is broken. APAC needs dedicated qualification support and deal-by-deal review entering Q2.
Paid Search produced 183 MQLs but only $480K Discovery — massive conversion gap
Paid Search was the single largest MQL channel (183 of 910, 30%) yet generated only $480K in Discovery pipeline — 5% of its $9.6M target. By comparison, Events produced 120 MQLs and $6.9M in Discovery. The cost-per-pipeline-dollar for Paid Search is orders of magnitude worse than Events. A full channel audit — lead quality, ICP match rates, and SDR response times — is urgent for Q2 budget allocation.
LinkedIn follower growth at 30% of target — 897 of 3,000
Social follower growth reached 897 against a 3,000 quarterly goal — just 30% attainment. Weekly gains averaged 30–40 net new followers, with a brief spike to 187 in late February. The social program needs a fundamentally different content strategy and cadence to reach the 3,000 goal. Consider leveraging event content, customer proof points, and executive thought leadership to drive follower acquisition in Q2.

Key takeaway: Week 12 was the quarter's weakest week across virtually every metric, confirming Q1 ended with zero momentum. MQLs cratered from 126 to just 14 — a 89% drop — with the Week 11 Events surge not sustaining. No Sales Accepted meetings were recorded. Meeting Booked ARR fell 82% to $208K, with all 4 new opps coming from APAC ($208K). Both Pre-Pipeline Discovery and Qualified Pipeline recorded zero new dollars. This was not unexpected — Week 12 typically suffers from end-of-quarter wind-down — but it means Q2 must start with an aggressive sprint. The $12.3M Discovery pipeline and 120 open Discovery opps represent a strong Q2 starting position if conversion velocity can be dramatically improved. The critical path is clear: convert Discovery to Qualified at pace in Weeks 1–4 of Q2 to avoid repeating Q1's structural gap.

This week
Recommended actions
1
Launch a "Q2 Week 1 Blitz" to convert the $12.3M Discovery pool into Qualified. There are 120 Discovery opps ($12.3M ARR) sitting in pre-pipeline — 68 in AMER alone ($8.0M). Identify the top 25 deals by ARR, assign executive sponsors by Tuesday, and set a target of moving at least $3M to Qualified in the first two weeks of Q2. The Q1 Discovery-to-Qualified conversion rate was 23% — even holding that rate would yield $2.8M, nearly matching Q1's entire Qualified total. Accelerating this to 35%+ would put Q2 on a completely different trajectory.
2
Conduct a full Q1 post-mortem on the MQL-to-Qualified conversion collapse. The 11% conversion rate (down from 18% in Q4) is the single biggest structural failure of Q1. With 910 MQLs producing only 24 Qualified opps, something is fundamentally broken. Audit by channel: Events MQLs (120) vs. Qualified attribution, Paid Search MQLs (183) vs. pipeline yield ($480K Discovery, near-zero Qualified). Determine whether this is a lead quality problem, a sales follow-up velocity problem, or a qualification criteria problem — and implement fixes before Q2 pipeline starts flowing.
3
Reallocate Q2 budget from underperforming channels to Events and Webinars. Events generated 76% of all Meeting Booked ARR ($18.7M) and reached 73% of its Discovery target. Webinars hit 50% projected of Discovery target. Meanwhile, Direct (3% of target), Inbound Paid (5%), and Email (6%) collectively underperformed catastrophically. For Q2, shift at minimum 20% of Paid Search and Direct budget into incremental event activations and webinar programming. The ROI data is unambiguous.
4
Fix APAC pipeline conversion — $3.1M in Meetings produced only $346K Qualified. APAC has the worst conversion funnel of any geo: $3.1M Meeting Booked → $1.8M Discovery → $346K Qualified (8% projected against $5.2M target). Conduct a deal-by-deal review of all 32 APAC Discovery opps this week. Establish APAC-specific qualification SLAs, assign regional executive sponsors to top deals, and consider whether the APAC sales team needs additional enablement or whether ICP targeting requires adjustment for the region.
5
Redesign Enterprise pipeline strategy — 10% Qualified attainment is a crisis. Enterprise Qualified finished at $894K — 10% of the $10.2M target. Mid-Market ($1.30M) outperformed despite having no assigned target, and Commercial ($666K) reached 48% of its smaller target. Enterprise requires a fundamentally different approach in Q2: dedicated Enterprise AE + SDR pods, named-account event activations (leveraging the proven Shoptalk/HIMSS/ViVE formats), and executive-sponsored outreach. Consider creating an Enterprise War Room with weekly deal progression reviews for all Enterprise Discovery opps over $200K.
