The final week of Q1 is in the books — 96% of the quarter elapsed — and four of five pipeline metrics finish behind target. MQLs closed at 1,056 of 2,770 (40% projected), the strongest relative metric of the quarter after Week 12 delivered 156 MQLs — the quarter's highest total — driven by a 30-MQL Email surge and 29 from Events. Meeting Booked # closed at 302 of 277 — 109% of target and the quarter's only metric to exceed its goal — with Week 12 adding 11 meetings. Meeting Booked ARR reached $28.6M (60% projected of $49.8M), adding $716K in the final full week. Pre-Pipeline Discovery closed at $13.8M (32% projected of $44.9M), with Week 12 adding $1.54M — led by Events at $628K and Digital Travel Connect's $468K debut. Qualified Pipeline actually declined in projected attainment from 21% to 18%, closing at $2.75M against the $15.7M target — a $12.9M gap. No new deals qualified in Week 12, making it the only zero-Qualified week since the quarter began. The AI-to-Comms Qualified ratio sits at 8.4:1 ($2.46M AI vs. $294K Comms). Events generated $18.7M in Meeting Booked ARR (76% of total) and $6.9M in Discovery (64% of total), cementing the channel as the quarter's dominant engine. Branded search volume hit 146% of target at 6,975 impressions. The quarter closes with a clear mandate: convert the $13.8M Discovery pool into Qualified pipeline in the opening weeks of Q2.

What's working
Wins & highlights
Week 12 MQLs hit 156 — the quarter's all-time high
156 MQLs · ▲24% WoW from 126
The final full week shattered the prior record of 126 set just one week earlier. Email channel exploded from 1 to 30 MQLs (a 29x increase), Events contributed 29 (down from 57 but still strong), Paid Search held steady at 18, and Direct surged to 16. The 156-MQL week pushed total quarter MQLs to 1,056 — crossing the 1,000 milestone. Contacts dominated at 132 (85%) versus 24 Leads, indicating re-engagement of existing database contacts is working.
Events channel delivered $18.7M in Meeting Booked ARR — 76% of total
$18.7M Events · $6.9M Discovery · $1.1M Qualified
Events is the undeniable Q1 engine. Shoptalk Spring led with $4.0M in Meeting Booked, followed by HIMSS ($3.6M), ViVE ($3.3M), eTail Palm Springs ($1.9M), and CCW Exchange UK ($1.4M). Events Discovery pipeline reached $6.9M — 73% projected against its $9.8M target, the strongest channel performance in the funnel. Events also generated $1.1M in Qualified pipeline, with CCW Orlando ($800K) and ViVE ($310K) as the top converters.
Branded search volume finished at 146% of target
6,975 actual vs. 5,000 target · 7,299 projected
Branded search impressions closed at 6,975 — nearly 50% above the 5,000 quarterly target with a projected full-quarter attainment of 7,299. This represents sustained brand awareness growth throughout Q1 and is a strong leading indicator for Q2 inbound pipeline. Weeks 1–4 averaged 635 impressions weekly, demonstrating consistent demand throughout the quarter.
Pre-Pipeline Discovery added $1.54M in Week 12 — late-quarter momentum
$13.8M total · $1.54M added in Wk 12
Week 12 added $1.54M to Discovery, up 9% from the prior week's $1.41M. Digital Travel Connect debuted with $468K, APAC KO Roundtable added $80K, and the DTC Winery event contributed $75K in new pipeline. Twelve Discovery opps entered the funnel in Week 12. AMER Discovery reached $9.1M (66% of total), while EMEA showed late strength with $2.0M total Discovery for the quarter.
LinkedIn followers gained 897 of 3,000 target — 30% attainment
897 new followers · 40 added in Week 12
While below the 3,000-follower quarterly goal, the organic follower growth has been steady, with 40 new followers in Week 12 and a weekly average of 34 across the quarter. The February spike of 187 followers in one week (likely tied to event promotions) showed what's possible with concentrated activity.
Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Qualified Pipeline closes Q1 at 18% — $12.9M gap to target
$2.75M actual (18%) · $2.88M projected of $15.7M target
Qualified Pipeline actually dropped in projected attainment from 21% to 18% as Week 12 delivered zero new Qualified deals — the only blank Qualified week of the quarter. The $12.9M shortfall is the quarter's defining miss. Enterprise Qualified stands at $874K (9% of $10.2M target). Marketing Programs sourced $1.62M (22% of $7.8M target), SDR Prospecting just $157K (5%), and Sales Prospecting $403K (13%). Only 24 opps qualified all quarter across all sources — structural changes are imperative for Q2.
Pre-Pipeline (Discovery) finishes at 32% — $31M below target
$13.8M actual (31%) · $14.4M projected of $44.9M
Despite $13.8M in absolute Discovery pipeline — a meaningful pool — attainment closed at just 32% of the $44.9M target. Direct channel Discovery is catastrophically low at $640K against an $18.4M target (4%). Inbound Paid reached only $480K of $9.6M (5%). Events ($6.9M, 73% projected) is the lone bright spot. APAC Discovery closed at $2.1M (15% of estimated target) and EMEA at $2.0M — both significantly underperforming AMER's $9.1M.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion rate remains at 11% — QoQ collapse
11% · ↓41% QoQ from 18% in Q4
Despite generating 1,056 MQLs, only 24 reached Qualified — an 11% conversion rate, down 41% from Q4's 18%. The funnel is producing top-of-funnel volume but failing to convert. Paid Search generated 199 MQLs (the single largest channel) yet contributed minimal downstream pipeline. The 156-MQL Week 12 will need to convert at materially higher rates in Q2 to justify current spend levels.
Meeting Booked # exceeded target; Meeting Booked $ remains behind
Mtg Booked #: 114% ↑4pp · Mtg Booked $: 60% ↓3pp
Both metrics dropped 3 percentage points WoW. SALs closed at 167 of 277 (60% projected), with Week 12 adding only 11 acceptances. Meeting Booked ARR finished at $28.6M of $49.8M (60% projected), with Week 12 contributing just $716K against the $3.45M weekly target. AMER leads at $19.9M (64% of $32.4M target), but APAC ($3.6M, 30%) and EMEA ($3.6M, 75%) diverge sharply in efficiency.
APAC Qualified stalled at $326K — 7% of $5.2M target
APAC generated $3.6M in Meeting Booked ARR and $2.1M in Discovery but only $326K reached Qualified (5 opps all quarter). The Discovery-to-Qualified conversion rate is under 16%. AICC ($543K Meeting Booked, $370K Discovery) and KO Roundtable ($522K Meeting Booked, $80K Discovery) produced volume but almost nothing qualified. APAC pipeline maturity is a critical Q2 priority.
Inbound Paid Discovery at 5% — $480K of $9.6M target
Paid Search generated 199 MQLs — the quarter's top MQL channel — yet produced only $480K in Discovery pipeline (5% of $9.6M target). This represents a fundamental disconnect between top-of-funnel Paid volume and downstream pipeline conversion. Events converts at 73% projected while Paid Search is at 5%. Budget reallocation or channel strategy overhaul is needed for Q2.
SDR + Sales Prospecting Qualified combined at just 8%
SDR Prospecting ($157K, 5% projected) and Sales Prospecting ($403K, 13% projected) together account for $560K of a $6.3M combined target. Marketing Programs sourced $1.69M at 22% projected — three times the combined outbound result. Outbound pipeline generation needs a fundamental reset for Q2, including revisiting ICP targeting, cadence design, and SDR-AE handoff processes.

Key takeaway: Week 12 delivered the quarter's best MQL week at 156 — up 24% from the prior record of 126 — driven by a 30-MQL Email surge and 29 from Events, suggesting late-quarter nurture campaigns and event follow-ups are bearing fruit at the top of funnel. However, everything below MQLs decelerated. Meeting Booked ARR was cut in half to $716K, Meeting Booked # added only 11 new meetings (down from 18), and — most critically — zero deals qualified, making it the first and only blank Qualified week of Q1. Pre-Pipeline Discovery was the lone mid-funnel bright spot, adding $1.54M (up 16% WoW) with new events like Digital Travel Connect ($468K) and APAC KO Roundtable ($80K) contributing fresh pipeline. The divergence between top-of-funnel momentum and mid-to-bottom-funnel stagnation is now the defining pattern of Q1. The 1,056 MQLs and $13.8M in Discovery represent a meaningful Q2 entrance pool, but converting it will require fundamentally faster qualification velocity and improved SDR/AE follow-up cadences. Q1 closes with all five metrics behind target — the strategic focus must now shift entirely to Q2 acceleration.

Launch a "Q2 Week 1 Blitz" to convert the $13.8M Discovery pool into Qualified pipeline. With $13.8M in Pre-Pipeline Discovery and only $2.75M Qualified, the conversion opportunity is massive. Prioritize the top 25 Discovery deals by ARR — especially from Shoptalk Spring ($1.1M Discovery), ViVE ($935K), and HIMSS ($440K) — assign executive sponsors, and target $3M+ in Qualified conversions in the first two weeks of Q2. The 132 active Discovery opps represent the strongest entrance pipeline available.

Redesign the Paid Search-to-pipeline conversion pathway for Q2. Paid Search was the #1 MQL channel (199 MQLs, 27% of total) but produced only $480K in Discovery — a 5% attainment rate versus Events at 73%. Conduct a full audit of Paid Search lead quality, ICP match rates, and SDR follow-up timing. Establish a dedicated Paid Search nurture track with faster SLA requirements. If conversion doesn't improve within 4 weeks of Q2, redirect budget to Events and Webinars where ROI is proven.
3
Double down on the Events engine for Q2 with locked investments by end of Week 1. Events generated $18.7M in Meeting Booked (76% of total), $6.9M in Discovery (64%), and $1.1M in Qualified. The top five events — Shoptalk Spring ($4.0M), HIMSS ($3.6M), ViVE ($3.3M), eTail Palm Springs ($1.9M), CCW Exchange UK ($1.4M) — provide clear format templates. Lock Q2 event sponsorships immediately. Begin pre-event outreach 8+ weeks out to compress the Discovery-to-Qualified timeline that plagued Q1.
4
Fix APAC pipeline maturity — convert $2.1M Discovery into Qualified. APAC produced $3.6M in Meeting Booked and $2.1M in Discovery but only $326K Qualified (7% projected). Conduct a deal-by-deal review of all 37 APAC Discovery opps, establish region-specific qualification SLAs (72-hour follow-up maximum), and assign dedicated APAC AE coverage for the top 10 deals. The AICC and KO Roundtable events generated strong pipeline that must not stall in early-stage limbo.
5
Restructure Enterprise pipeline strategy — 9% attainment is a crisis. Enterprise Qualified finished at $874K of $10.2M target (9%). Mid-Market ($1.30M) outperformed on a per-segment basis. For Q2: create dedicated Enterprise AE + SDR pods, deploy named-account event activations at every major conference, and launch executive-sponsored outreach to the top 50 Enterprise accounts. Consider reducing the Enterprise target or fundamentally restructuring the go-to-market approach — 9% attainment cannot repeat.
6
Capitalize on the 156-MQL Week 12 with accelerated follow-up. Week 12's record MQL volume — including 30 from Email and 29 from Events — represents the quarter's highest-quality batch of marketing-generated leads. Ensure 100% SDR follow-up within 24 hours for this cohort. The 30 Email MQLs suggest nurture campaigns are re-engaging dormant contacts; build a "Q2 Fast Track" for these high-intent re-engaged leads to prevent them from entering the same slow qualification cycle that produced Q1's 11% conversion rate.
