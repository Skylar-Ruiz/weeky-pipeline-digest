Monday, March 23, 2026 · FY2027 Q1 · Week 8 of 13

Summary

The 30-day email portfolio continues to outperform benchmarks on engagement while flagging sustained pressure on unsubscribes. Open rate stands at 34.0%, comfortably above the 31.35% benchmark, and click-through rate of 5.0% runs nearly 2× the industry standard of 2.78%. The week ending March 23 saw the highest open rate of the window at 80.0% (small-volume sends) and a robust 12.5% CTR. The March newsletter was the standout program, reaching 38,472 contacts with a 39.72% open rate and 4.82% CTR. The persistent concern: unsubscribe rate at 0.35% is 2.3× the 0.15% benchmark, driven heavily by the Delight Spark event series. Delivered rate holds strong at 98% and bounce rate sits right at the 1.0% target.

Wins, Concerns & Watch Items
✓
Wins
Newsletter open rate rebounded to 39.72% after February's dip to 34.63%, returning to January-level engagement on 38,472 delivered contacts. CTR of 4.82% remains nearly 2× the industry benchmark. The AI Readiness Webinar program is a clear second engine: 37.95% open rate and 4.92% CTR across 8,230 sends, demonstrating that educational content resonates with this audience. Delivery rate improved to 98%, and bounce rate tightened to exactly 1.0%, right at target. Conference booth follow-ups (ViVE 45.28%, HIMSS 45.26%, eTail 36.84% open rates) confirm that in-person touchpoints convert exceptionally well to email engagement.
!
Concerns
Unsubscribe rate of 0.35% remains 2.3× above the 0.15% benchmark. The Spark event invite series continues to be the primary driver: its 14,780-person send carried a 0.40% unsub rate with 56 total unsubscribes and 387 bounces (2.62% bounce rate) — suggesting both stale contacts and audience fatigue in the event database. The ViVE booth follow-up also hit a concerning 2.52% unsubscribe rate on 159 delivered, indicating the post-booth messaging may feel too aggressive for casual booth visitors. Additionally, the Spark Invite 3 open rate of 11.63% to 13,720 recipients is the lowest in the entire portfolio — nearly every other email type performs 2–4× better.
⚠
Watch Items
Newsletter CTR is trending down: Jan 5.27% → Feb 6.90% → Mar 4.82%. While still well above benchmark, March's drop reflects a shift in content mix — the February issue benefited from the Spark event CTA which drove 899 clicks alone. Without a comparable conversion-focused CTA, March relied on thought leadership and customer stories, which drive awareness but fewer clicks per recipient. The AMER vs. EMEA CTR gap persists: AMER achieved 5.06% CTR vs. EMEA's 3.47% — a 1.59pp gap despite nearly identical open rates (39.73% vs. 39.64%). This suggests CTA placement or content relevance needs regional tuning. Finally, list size is contracting: AMER newsletter sends dropped from 38,089 (Jan) to 32,872 (Mar), a −13.7% decline over three months that warrants investigation into list growth and suppression policies.
Recommended Actions
1
Scrub and suppress the Spark event list before any further sends. The three-touch Spark series has accumulated 56 unsubscribes and 387 bounces on 14,780 sends — a combined opt-out and bounce rate of 3.0%. Before sending Invite 4 or any reminder, remove all bounced addresses, suppress non-openers from Invites 1–3, and apply engagement scoring to restrict the remaining list to contacts who opened at least one prior email. This single action should meaningfully lower both the portfolio unsubscribe and bounce rates.
2
Add a high-intent CTA to the April newsletter to recover CTR. March's CTR dropped to 4.82% from February's 6.90%, largely because February had the Spark event page CTA which alone drove 899 clicks. For April, embed a single prominent conversion-focused CTA — such as the on-demand webinar replay or a product demo booking — above the fold to complement the thought leadership and customer story content that already drives strong open rates.
3
Investigate and address the AMER list contraction. AMER newsletter sends have declined from 38,089 (January) to 32,872 (March) — a 13.7% loss of 5,217 addressable contacts in three months. Audit whether this is driven by suppression rules, unsubscribes accumulating quarter-over-quarter, or a gap in net-new lead acquisition. If suppression policies are the cause, consider re-engagement campaigns for dormant contacts before permanent removal
