Nine weeks in — 64% of Q1 elapsed — and the funnel shows meaningful downstream progress but remains well short of pace across every stage. SALs have been the biggest mover, jumping from 35% to 55% projected as the team delivered 98 meetings (35% of target) — a dramatic improvement in conversion cadence. Meeting Booked ARR climbed to $18.0M (56% projected) and Pre-Pipeline reached $6.8M (23% projected), both showing real momentum. But MQLs have slipped to 37% projected — below the Week 6 reading of 39% — signaling top-of-funnel deceleration. Qualified Pipeline at $1.8M (18% projected) against a $15.7M target remains the quarter's critical bottleneck. Events continue as the dominant engine with $3.2M in Pre-Pipeline Discovery ARR, and AI pipeline leads Comms 11.5:1 at the Qualified stage. With four weeks remaining, the gap between volume generated and pipeline qualified is the defining challenge.

What's working
Wins & highlights
SAL pacing surged from 35% to 55% projected
98 SALs · +20pp improvement
The SAL bottleneck flagged in prior weeks has materially improved. Week 8 delivered 16 Sales Accepted meetings — the joint-highest week this quarter — and the team has now logged 98 against a 277 target (35% actual). The MQL-to-SAL handoff is finally responding.
Meeting Booked ARR hit $18.0M — the strongest cumulative read
$18.0M actual · 56% projected
Meeting Booked jumped +16pp in projected attainment. Week 8 alone delivered $1.86M in new Pre-Pipeline ARR led by AMER ($1.47M) and APAC ($240K). Events-attributed campaigns like HIMSS ($3.6M cumulative) and ViVE ($3.3M) are converting at scale.
Pre-Pipeline Discovery nearly doubled in projected pacing
$6.8M actual · 23% projected (was 14%)
Week 8 was the quarter's biggest Discovery week at $1.76M, driven by AMER ($930K) and EMEA ($750K). EMEA's first significant Discovery contribution — $1.3M cumulative — is a breakout development. Total Discovery opps reached 63.
AI Qualified pipeline dominance widens to 11.5:1
$1.68M AI vs. $147K Comms Qualified
AI Qualified ARR now represents 92% of all Qualified pipeline. The ratio expanded from 6.7:1 three weeks ago, confirming the AI offering as the primary pipeline driver this quarter.
Branded search volume tracking to 152% of target
4,882 actual · 7,576 projected vs. 5,000 target
The only OKR metric pacing well above plan. Weekly impressions have held above 600 for seven consecutive weeks, signaling strong brand awareness momentum.
Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Qualified Pipeline at 18% projected — the quarter's most critical gap
$1.8M actual (12%) · $2.8M projected of $15.7M target
Even with downstream improvements, Qualified is tracking to miss by over $12.8M. Only 15 deals have reached Qualified stage all quarter. At the current weekly run-rate of ~$240K, the team would need $3.5M per week over the final 4 weeks to hit target — a 15x acceleration.
Enterprise Qualified still deeply distressed at 4% projected
$284K actual · $441K projected of $10.2M target
Enterprise holds 65% of the Qualified target and has produced just $284K — less than 3% of plan. The segment has generated only $284K cumulative across 8 weeks. Mid-Market ($1.22M) has quietly become the workhorse, but cannot compensate for this Enterprise shortfall.
MQL pacing declined from 39% to 37% projected
668 actual (24%) · 1,037 projected of 2,770 target
Top-of-funnel is decelerating. Week 8 delivered 76 MQLs, down from the 153 peak in Week 5. Paid Search (141 MQLs) and Organic Search (92) remain the primary drivers, but volume is not keeping up with the target trajectory.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion rate still at 9% — unchanged
9% · ↓48% QoQ
Despite SAL improvement, the quality-to-qualification funnel hasn't budged from the 9% rate flagged three weeks ago. Down from 18% in FY26 Q4, this efficiency drag continues to compound every upstream gain.
SDR Prospecting Qualified at 7% projected — weakest source
$150K actual ($233K projected) against a $3.1M target. Only one deal qualified from SDR Prospecting all quarter. Sales Prospecting at 18% and Marketing Programs at 17% are modestly better but all sources underperform.
APAC Qualified improved but still at 6% projected
$210K actual ($326K projected) against a $5.2M target. APAC added $157K in Week 7–8 after a prolonged drought, but the region remains deeply behind. EMEA has produced just $150K Qualified all quarter.
Social followers at 25% of target — unlikely to recover
752 followers gained against a 3,000 Q1 target. Weekly gains have dropped to 25–49 range after sporadic spikes. At current pace, the team will finish well under 50% of goal.
