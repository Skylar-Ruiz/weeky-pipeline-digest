Key historical insights:

1. FY27 Q1 is a generational quarter for events. At $17.9M Meeting Booked with one week left, this quarter has produced 25× the Meeting Booked ARR of any prior quarter. The concentrated tier-1 calendar (ViVE, HIMSS, Shoptalk Spring, eTail PS) drove this surge, but the Pre-pipeline conversion challenge means much of this value remains trapped early in the funnel.

2. Conversion rates are compressing under volume pressure. The Pre-pipeline-to-Qualified rate sits at 24% this quarter vs. 38% in FY26 Q4. Meanwhile, only 433 of 772 opps (56%) have reached Pre-pipeline stage — 339 opps remain stuck before Pre-pipeline, indicating a processing bottleneck rather than a quality issue.

3. Best ROI benchmarks remain small-format events. Boston Yacht Dinner (FY26 Q2) delivered 8.7× ROI on $7.5K spend. CCW Vegas achieved 1.7× on $91K. Field dinners (Ariat Nashville, Columbus CX) showed strong conversion at minimal cost. These formats remain the most capital-efficient path to pipeline, even as conference-scale events dominate raw volume.

Wins, Concerns & Watch Items

✦ Wins
Shoptalk Spring broke through with $443K in Pre-pipeline this week — the largest single-week Pre-pipeline add for any event this quarter — bringing its total to $1.12M (29% of Meeting Booked). This validates the pipeline is progressing, just with a lag. ViVE remains the standout event at $1.04M Pre-pipeline and $430K Qualified from $3.4M Meeting Booked (31% Pre-pipeline conversion, the best among tier-1 conferences). AICC (APAC) continues to punch above its weight with $370K Pre-pipeline from $502K Meeting Booked — a 74% conversion rate, the highest in the portfolio.


✦ Concerns
HIMSS is the largest conversion gap in the portfolio. With $3.65M Meeting Booked and only $440K Pre-pipeline (12% conversion), 38 of 43 opps have not reached Pre-pipeline stage. Only 5 opps have progressed to Pre-pipeline with zero reaching Qualified — the worst conversion rate among top events. Fintech Meetup generated $924K Meeting Booked with zero Pre-pipeline — 0 of 11 opps are in Pre-pipeline stage. This event's pipeline is rapidly aging without progression. Qualified Pipeline had zero movement this week — a complete stall after $352K last week. The $1.17M QTD Qualified total remains entirely dependent on ViVE ($430K) and CCW Orlando ($740K).


✦ Watch Items
KO Roundtable (APAC) added $294K Meeting Booked but shows zero Pre-pipeline movement with only 2 of 13 opps progressed (15%) — it needs immediate follow-up before quarter close. CCW Orlando (FY26 Q4 carryover) still holds the largest Qualified balance at $740K across 6 opps — these must be protected in active close plans. Week-over-week velocity is declining across all three stages simultaneously for the first time this quarter, signaling end-of-quarter fatigue in the final week.

Recommended Actions
1
All-hands push on HIMSS progression — final week. $3.65M in Meeting Booked with only 12% converted to Pre-pipeline. With 38 opps stuck before Pre-pipeline, this is the single largest lever to close the $3.89M gap. Coordinate a dedicated blitz this week: assign named SDR coverage to each of the 38 stalled opps, send day-1 personal outreach from event attendees, and escalate the top 10 accounts by ARR to AEs for direct scheduling.
2
Rescue Fintech Meetup before pipeline goes cold. $924K Meeting Booked with zero Pre-pipeline movement — 11 opps completely stalled. These opps are now 2+ weeks old with no follow-up. Run an emergency email sequence this week and have the attending AE re-engage the top 5 contacts by deal size. If opps cannot be progressed, flag for deprioritization to free rep capacity.
3
Sustain Shoptalk Spring momentum. The $443K Pre-pipeline add this week shows conversion is accelerating, but 38 of 53 opps (72%) still haven't reached Pre-pipeline. With $3.9M Meeting Booked, even a 10% incremental conversion would add ~$270K. Prioritize the remaining high-value opps for Discovery calls this week to capitalize on the momentum before Q1 closes.
4
Protect the $1.17M Qualified pipeline for Q1 close. Qualified had zero movement this week. The $740K CCW Orlando balance (6 opps) and $430K ViVE balance (3 opps) represent the entire Qualified portfolio. Ensure each of these 9 opps has an active close plan with executive sponsorship and next steps confirmed before April 25. Any slippage here directly impacts the quarter.
5
Begin Q2 events planning debrief. With Q1 wrapping, catalog lessons: ViVE and AICC demonstrated that mid-quarter timing and smaller attendee counts yield higher conversion rates (31% and 74% respectively) vs. late-quarter mega-events like HIMSS and Shoptalk (12% and 29%). Factor this into FY27 Q2 event selection and budget allocation — front-load events in weeks 2–8 to allow conversion time.
