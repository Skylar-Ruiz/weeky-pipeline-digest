Eleven weeks in — 85% of Q1 elapsed — and the quarter is entering its final stretch with a sharply mixed picture. Mid-funnel conversion has dramatically improved: SALs surged from 35% to 65% projected (147 actual, 53% of 277 target), and Meeting Booked ARR jumped to 66% projected ($26.5M of $49.8M). Pre-Pipeline more than doubled its projection from 14% to 31% ($14.1M actual). Qualified Pipeline climbed to 21% projected ($2.6M of $15.7M) — still behind, but a meaningful step forward. However, MQLs deteriorated to 36% projected (800 of 2,770), declining for the first time this quarter as Week 10 delivered just 33 MQLs — the lowest since Week 1. Events remain the dominant pipeline engine at $17.4M in attributed Meeting Booked ARR, accounting for 79% of all pipeline created. The AI-to-Comms Qualified ratio stands at 16.3:1 ($2.49M AI vs. $152K Comms). With only two weeks remaining, closing velocity is everything.

What's working

147 SALs · 65% projected · ▲30pp
Sales Accepted meetings leapt from 40 actual at Week 6 to 147 — a 268% increase over five weeks. Week ending Apr 11 delivered 24 SALs, the quarter's strongest week. The MQL-to-SAL handoff that was previously broken has now corrected decisively.

Meeting Booked ARR topped $26.5M — AMER at $19.4M
$26.5M actual · 66% projected
Pre-Pipeline generation (Meetings Booked) accelerated sharply, nearly tripling since Week 6. AMER accounts for 73% at $19.4M, with EMEA contributing $3.3M. Shoptalk Spring alone generated $3.9M and Fintech Meetup added $924K in recent weeks.
EMEA Pre-Pipeline emerging — $1.83M in Discovery
82% projected attainment on Opp Created
EMEA's projected attainment on pipeline created is now 82%, up sharply from earlier in the quarter. CCW Exchange UK ($575K Discovery) and ViVE follow-on activity are pulling EMEA into contention. EMEA Qualified remains at $150K but directional momentum is strong.

The partial Week 11 shows only 19. The top-of-funnel is losing steam precisely when downstream conversion is finally working.

Despite improvement, Qualified Pipeline is tracking to miss its target by nearly 80%. With only two weeks left, the gap of $13.1M is insurmountable. Enterprise Qualified at $894K (11% of $10.2M target) and APAC Qualified at $352K (8% of $5.2M) remain the weakest segments.

$185K projected of a $3.1M target. SDR-sourced pipeline is barely registering in Qualified, compared to Marketing Programs at 23% ($1.83M). SDR effectiveness needs a structural review heading into Q2.

Key takeaway: Week 10 was a tale of downstream momentum vs. upstream softening. Pre-Pipeline (Discovery) ARR surged 159% to $2.94M — the quarter's best week — driven by $1.46M in Events Discovery and $179K in Other. Discovery opps more than doubled to 31. Qualified came alive at $470K after a zero week, with 4 opps qualifying including AMER contributions of $350K. SALs had their strongest week at 24. But Meeting Booked ARR fell 72% as the post-events surge normalized, and MQLs continued declining (63 → 52). The partial Week 11 (ending Apr 18) shows further deceleration with only 4 meetings booked and 19 MQLs thus far. The quarter's final push must prioritize converting the $11.4M sitting in Discovery into Qualified pipeline.
