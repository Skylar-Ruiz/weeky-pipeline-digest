Key historical insights:

1. FY27 Q1 is a breakout quarter for events volume. At $10.6M in Meeting Booked pipeline (with 2 weeks left), this quarter is already 6× the total pipeline of any prior quarter — driven by a concentrated calendar of tier-1 conferences (ViVE, HIMSS, Shoptalk Spring, eTail PS).

2. Conversion rates are compressing under volume. Pre-pipeline-to-Discovery sits at 44% and Discovery-to-Qualified at 24% this quarter versus 38% in FY26 Q4. The sheer volume of new opps is outpacing the team's ability to progress them — 332 opps remain stuck before Pre-pipeline.

3. Best cost-efficiency benchmark: CCW Vegas (FY26 Q2) at 1.7× ROI on $91K spend and Boston Yacht Dinner at 8.7× ROI on $7.5K — both small-format events with high conversion. The FY26 Q3 field dinners (Ariat Nashville, Columbus CX) also showed strong conversion at low cost.

Wins, Concerns & Watch Items

✦ Wins
ViVE is the top-performing event of the quarter with $942K in Pre-pipeline from $3.4M Meeting Booked (28% conversion) and $430K already Qualified — the only event with meaningful Qualified movement. CCW Exchange UK converted 45% of Meeting Booked to Pre-pipeline ($576K), the highest conversion rate among major events. AICC (APAC) quietly delivered $370K in Pre-pipeline from only $400K Meeting Booked — a 93% conversion rate, the best in the portfolio.


✦ Concerns
Shoptalk Spring and HIMSS together represent $7.4M in Meeting Booked but only $1.12M has moved to Pre-pipeline (15% conversion). Both had late-quarter timing, but with only 2 weeks remaining, the window to convert is closing fast. Fintech Meetup generated $1.03M Meeting Booked but has only $110K Pre-pipeline and just 1 of 12 opps in Discovery — an 8% stage conversion rate, the lowest among top events. Digital Travel Connect ($410K Meeting Booked, zero Pre-pipeline) and DTC Winery ($75K, zero) show no pipeline progression at all.


✦ Watch Items
CCW Orlando (FY26 Q4 carryover) is an outlier: only $82K in Meeting Booked but $928K in Pre-pipeline and $740K Qualified — indicating high-quality pipeline from a prior event that is still progressing. Ensure these opps are being actively worked. MQL-to-Qualified rate across all channels dropped to 8% this quarter (from 18% in FY26 Q4, a −57% decline) — events should monitor whether this compression extends to their funnel.

Recommended Actions
1
Sprint on Shoptalk Spring & HIMSS progression. Combined $7.4M in Meeting Booked with only 15% converted to Pre-pipeline. With 2 weeks left in Q1, these represent the largest lever to close the $4.3M Pre-pipeline gap. Coordinate with field teams to schedule Discovery calls for the 84 open opps (52 Shoptalk + 43 HIMSS) that haven't moved to Pre-pipeline.
2
Rescue Fintech Meetup pipeline. $1.03M Meeting Booked with only 1 of 12 opps progressed (8% rate). This event's pipeline is at risk of going cold. Assign dedicated SDR coverage this week and run a targeted email sequence to the 11 stalled contacts.
3
Escalate Digital Travel Connect and DTC Winery for field review. Combined $485K in Meeting Booked with zero Pre-pipeline movement and 0% stage conversion. Determine if opps are viable or should be deprioritized to free rep capacity for higher-potential accounts.
4
Protect CCW Orlando Qualified pipeline. $740K in Qualified from a prior-quarter event — the largest Qualified balance in the events portfolio. Ensure these 6 opps have active close plans and executive sponsorship before they age out of the pipeline.
