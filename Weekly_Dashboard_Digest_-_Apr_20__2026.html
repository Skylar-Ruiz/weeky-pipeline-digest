Twelve weeks in — 88% of Q1 elapsed — and the quarter is effectively over with one reporting week remaining. Projections have flatlined across nearly every metric, with no meaningful acceleration despite a strong Week 11 MQL spike. MQLs ticked up to 37% projected (910 of 2,770) after Week 11 delivered 126 MQLs — the quarter's single best week — largely driven by a 56-MQL Events surge and 13 from Paid Search. SALs slipped slightly to 63% projected (154 actual, 56% of 277 target) as Week 11 added just 11 acceptances. Meeting Booked ARR reached $27.7M (63% projected), while Pre-Pipeline held steady at $12.3M (31% projected) and Qualified Pipeline inched to $2.9M (21% projected). Events remain the dominant engine at $17.9M in Meeting Booked ARR (78% of total). The Qualified Pipeline gap is now $12.8M — insurmountable in the final week. The AI-to-Comms Qualified ratio widened to 6.5:1 ($2.48M AI vs. $378K Comms). Week 12's partial data shows just 5 MQLs and $208K in Meeting Booked, confirming the quarter will close well below target. Focus must now shift decisively to Q2 readiness.

What's working
Wins & highlights
Week 11 MQLs surged to 126 — the quarter's best week by far
126 MQLs · ▲142% WoW from 52
After weeks of decline, Week 11 (ending Apr 18) exploded to 126 MQLs — more than double the prior week's 52 and surpassing the previous high of 100 (Week 4). Events drove 56 MQLs (up from 1 the prior week), likely fueled by APAC roundtable registrations (7 MQLs) and late-quarter event follow-ups. Organic Search contributed 11 and Paid Search 13, both recovering from lows.
Qualified Pipeline crossed $2.86M — Week 11 delivered $602K
$2.86M actual · $602K in Week 11
Week 11 was the quarter's strongest Qualified week at $602K across 5 opps, pushing total Qualified to $2.86M. SACA registered its first Qualified deal at $150K, and an Unknown-geo deal added $226K. AMER continues to lead with $1.98M (69% of total). Marketing Programs sourced $1.70M of total Qualified — 60% of the pipeline.
Meeting Booked ARR reached $27.7M — 56% of target
$27.7M actual · $1.18M added in Wk 11
Despite a quieter week, Meeting Booked ARR added $1.18M in Week 11, pushing the quarter total to $27.7M. APAC contributed $415K and SACA added $150K — both showing late-quarter signs of life. The cumulative total represents $27.7M in net-new pipeline entering the funnel this quarter.
Branded search volume already at 147% of target
6,467 actual vs. 5,000 target · 147% projected
Branded search impressions have significantly outperformed at 6,467 against a 5,000 quarterly target, projecting to 7,367 by quarter-end. This is a strong leading indicator of growing brand awareness and will feed downstream organic and direct pipeline into Q2.
Events Discovery pipeline at 69% projected — $6.78M
$5.95M actual · 60% of $9.8M target
Events channel Discovery (Pre-Pipeline) is the standout at $5.95M actual against a $9.8M target — 60% attained with 69% projected. This massively outperforms every other channel. Webinars also showing strength at $494K actual (51% projected of target). The Events engine continues to validate as the primary pipeline accelerator.
Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Qualified Pipeline finishing at ~21% — $12.8M gap is insurmountable
$2.86M actual (18%) · $3.25M projected of $15.7M target
Despite the best Qualified week of the quarter ($602K), the projected attainment remains stuck at 21%. The $12.8M gap to target cannot be closed in one week. Enterprise Qualified stands at just $894K (10% of $10.2M target), and SDR Prospecting sourced only $150K of $3.1M target (5% projected). This is the quarter's most critical miss and demands a structural reset in Q2 planning.
Pre-Pipeline (Discovery) stalled at 31% — $12.3M of $44.9M
$12.3M actual (28%) · $14.1M projected
Pre-Pipeline Discovery has flatlined at 31% projected after briefly surging. Week 11 added $1.41M — a significant deceleration from Week 10's $2.94M. APAC Discovery went to zero in Week 11, and EMEA also recorded nothing. Direct channel Discovery is at just 3% of its $18.4M target ($573K actual) — a staggering underperformance that points to fundamental issues with non-event pipeline generation.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion rate collapsed to 11%
11% · ↓40% QoQ from 18% in Q4
The MQL-to-Qualified conversion rate sits at 11% for Q1 — a 40% decline from Q4's 18%. Despite generating 910 MQLs, only 24 have reached Qualified stage. The funnel is producing top-of-funnel volume but failing to convert at historical rates, indicating potential issues with lead quality, sales follow-up velocity, or qualification criteria misalignment.
Meeting Booked projections declining — 63% down from 66%
63% projected · ↓3pp from last week
Meeting Booked projected attainment slipped from 66% to 63% as Week 11 added only $1.18M versus the $3.45M weekly target needed. The partial Week 12 shows just $208K. AMER projects 69% ($22.4M of $32.4M) and is the only region with reasonable trajectory — APAC is at 28% and SACA negligible.
APAC Qualified still anemic — $346K of $5.2M (8% projected)
APAC has generated $1.82M in Discovery and $3.09M in Meetings Booked but only $346K has reached Qualified (5 opps in 12 weeks). The conversion rate from Discovery to Qualified is among the lowest in the funnel. With Q1 effectively over, APAC pipeline maturity must be a Q2 priority.
Inbound Paid Discovery at 3% — $281K of $9.6M target
Paid Search generated 183 MQLs (30% of all MQLs) — the single largest MQL channel — yet produced only $281K in Discovery pipeline (3% of its $9.6M target). The disconnect between top-of-funnel Paid volume and downstream pipeline conversion is the quarter's biggest channel efficiency gap and needs investigation for Q2.
SDR and Sales Prospecting Qualified combined at just 8%
SDR Prospecting ($150K, 5% projected) and Sales Prospecting ($405K, 15% projected) together account for $555K of a $6.3M combined target. Marketing Programs sourced $1.70M at 25% projected — three times more effective. Outbound effectiveness must be fundamentally re-evaluated for Q2.

Key takeaway: Week 11 was a mixed bag — the strongest MQL and Qualified weeks of the quarter, but with meaningful deceleration everywhere else. MQLs spiked to 126 (up 142%), powered by a 56-MQL Events burst, but this likely reflects batch event-list imports rather than sustained demand. Pre-Pipeline Discovery fell 52% from $2.94M to $1.41M as the post-events surge faded. Meeting Booked ARR declined 31% to $1.18M. SALs dropped sharply from 25 to 11. The bright spot was Qualified Pipeline, which posted $602K — the best week of the quarter — across 5 opps including the first SACA deal ($150K) and a $226K Unknown-geo opp. The partial Week 12 data shows extreme deceleration: just 5 MQLs, $208K in Meeting Booked, and 4 meetings. The quarter will close next week with all five pipeline metrics behind target. Attention should now pivot firmly to Q2 planning and converting the $12.3M Discovery pool into Qualified pipeline early next quarter.

This week
Recommended actions
1
Convert the $12.3M Discovery pool into Qualified — this is the Q2 starting gun. With $12.3M in Pre-Pipeline and only $2.9M Qualified, there is a massive conversion opportunity. Prioritize the top 20 Discovery deals by ARR, assign executive sponsors, and create a "Week 1 Q2 Blitz" targeting at least $2M in Qualified conversions in the first two weeks of Q2. The 120 Discovery opps — 68 in AMER alone — represent the strongest Q2 entrance pipeline we've had.
2
Diagnose the Paid Search conversion collapse for Q2 remediation. Paid Search produced 183 MQLs (30% of total) but only $281K in Discovery pipeline — a 0.3% conversion rate to downstream stages versus Events at 50%+. Conduct a full audit of Paid Search lead quality, ICP match rates, and SDR follow-up timing. If the channel can't produce pipeline, reallocate budget to higher-converting channels like Events and Webinars.
3
Build the Q2 Events calendar around proven ROI data. Events generated $17.9M in Meeting Booked ARR (78% of total), $5.95M in Discovery (67% of total), and $1.17M in Qualified this quarter. Shoptalk Spring ($3.9M), ViVE ($3.4M), HIMSS ($3.6M), and CCW Exchange UK ($1.3M) are the proven formats. Lock Q2 event investments immediately and begin pre-event prospect outreach 6+ weeks ahead to accelerate the conversion timeline.
4
Overhaul APAC pipeline conversion strategy for Q2. APAC generated $3.1M in Meetings Booked and $1.82M in Discovery but only $346K Qualified — an 8% conversion rate from Discovery. With the APAC AICC event generating $502K in Meeting Booked and $370K in Discovery, the activity is there but qualification is broken. Conduct a deal-by-deal review of all 32 APAC Discovery opps and establish APAC-specific qualification SLAs.
5
Address Enterprise pipeline crisis in Q2 planning. Enterprise Qualified is at $894K — 10% of the $10.2M target. Mid-Market ($1.30M) and Commercial ($666K) are both outperforming on a relative basis. The Enterprise segment needs a fundamentally different approach: consider dedicated Enterprise AE + SDR pods, named-account event activations, and executive-sponsored outreach programs. Enterprise cannot remain at 10% attainment in Q2.
