Summary

FY27 Q1 closes this week with Events delivering $18.7M in Meeting Booked across 773 opps — the most productive events quarter on record. While the quarter fell short of its Pre-pipeline target by ~$2.9M, the sheer volume of early-stage pipeline — 334 opps still in pre-Discovery stages — represents a massive carryover opportunity for Q2 if conversion can be accelerated.

Key historical insights:

1. FY27 Q1 was a breakout quarter for events pipeline generation. At $18.7M Meeting Booked, this quarter produced 27× the Meeting Booked ARR of any prior quarter. The concentrated tier-1 conference calendar (ViVE, HIMSS, Shoptalk Spring, eTail PS) drove this surge, but the 70% Pre-pipeline attainment reveals a structural conversion bottleneck: the funnel cannot process this volume at historic rates.

2. Conversion rates compress as volume scales. Pre-pipeline-to-Qualified rate sits at 24% this quarter vs. 38% in FY26 Q4 — a 41% decline. Only 439 of 773 opps (57%) reached Pre-pipeline, leaving 334 opps unconverted as carryover into Q2. The top five events alone account for $14.1M in Meeting Booked but only $3.8M in Pre-pipeline (27% conversion). At the FY26 Q4 conversion rate, these same events would have generated $5.4M — a $1.6M conversion gap.

3. Small-format events deliver the highest ROI. Boston Yacht Dinner (FY26 Q2) produced 8.7× ROI on $7.5K spend. Shoptalk Chicago achieved 3.8× on $17.7K. McKinsey Alumni event delivered 50% Qualified-to-Won conversion at $2.4K cost. These formats remain the most capital-efficient path to pipeline — averaging 5.5× ROI vs. 1.4× for large conferences with known costs.

Wins, Concerns & Watch Items

✦ Wins
Digital Travel Connect converted 100% of Meeting Booked to Pre-pipeline in its final week — $468K moved from $468K Meeting Booked, the only event to achieve complete conversion this quarter. ViVE finished as the best-converting tier-1 event at $935K Pre-pipeline from $3.28M Meeting Booked (29% conversion) with $310K already in Qualified — a full-funnel success story. AICC (APAC) ended the quarter at 68% Pre-pipeline conversion ($370K from $543K Meeting Booked), the highest conversion rate among events with >$500K Meeting Booked. eTail Palm Springs reached 39% conversion ($728K from $1.86M Meeting Booked), crossing the $700K Pre-pipeline threshold in the final stretch. Pre-pipeline held steady in the final week at $628K, matching the prior week's pace and avoiding the end-of-quarter collapse seen in prior quarters.


✦ Concerns
HIMSS closes Q1 as the largest conversion gap in the portfolio. $3.65M Meeting Booked with only $440K Pre-pipeline (12% conversion) — 38 of 43 opps never reached Pre-pipeline this quarter. With zero Qualified, this event's $3.2M in unconverted Meeting Booked is the single largest carryover risk for Q2. KO Roundtable generated $522K Meeting Booked but only $80K Pre-pipeline (15% conversion) — 10 of 15 opps remain stuck in early stages. Qualified Pipeline stalled for two consecutive weeks — the final Qualified movement was $352K in the week ending April 11. The $1.1M Q1 total came from just three events: ViVE ($310K), CCW Orlando ($800K), and Spark Delight APAC ($3.9K). The quarter missed its Pre-pipeline target by $2.9M (70% attainment), the widest gap of any channel to its Q1 OKR.


✦ Watch Items
334 opps carrying $11.8M in unconverted Meeting Booked roll into Q2 — this represents both the biggest risk and biggest opportunity. If these convert at even the Q1 average rate (37% of Meeting Booked → Pre-pipeline), Q2 starts with ~$4.4M in Pre-pipeline from carryover alone. Fintech Meetup's 12 opps ($1.03M) are now 3+ weeks old with only $110K in Pre-pipeline — conversion probability decays rapidly past 4 weeks based on historical patterns. Shoptalk Spring holds $2.87M in unconverted Meeting Booked (28% of its total) across 38 opps — the largest single-event carryover pool requiring immediate Q2 action.

Recommended Actions
1
Launch a dedicated HIMSS conversion sprint in Q2 Week 1. $3.2M in unconverted Meeting Booked with 38 stalled opps is the single largest pipeline lever going into Q2. Assign named SDR coverage to each of the 38 opps, prioritized by deal size. Historical data shows conversion probability drops below 15% after 6 weeks — these opps were created in mid-March, making the next 2 weeks the critical window. Set a target of converting 10 opps ($800K+) to Pre-pipeline by May 8.
2
Triage Fintech Meetup and KO Roundtable before pipeline ages out. Combined $1.55M in Meeting Booked with only $190K in Pre-pipeline across 27 opps. These events are 3–4 weeks old. Run a joint triage session this week: classify each opp as "rescue" (active re-engagement) or "deprioritize" (free rep capacity). For rescue opps, deploy personalized outreach from the attending AE with event-specific context. Target: move 8+ opps to Pre-pipeline or flag for disqualification by May 2.
3
Accelerate the $1.1M Qualified pipeline into active close plans. Qualified had zero movement for two consecutive weeks. The ViVE ($310K, 3 opps) and CCW Orlando ($800K, 6 opps) Qualified balances represent the entire near-term revenue opportunity from events. Ensure each of these 9 opps has confirmed next steps, executive sponsorship, and close timelines documented in Salesforce by end of this week. Any opp without a scheduled meeting should be escalated to sales leadership.
4
Conduct a Q1 events retrospective and apply learnings to Q2 planning. Key takeaway: conversion rates correlate inversely with event size and timing. AICC (68% conversion), Digital Travel Connect (100%), and Sullivan's eTail PS (100%) — all smaller, mid-quarter events — dramatically outperformed HIMSS (12%) and Shoptalk (28%) on conversion. For Q2, front-load events in weeks 2–8 to allow conversion time, and cap the tier-1 conference count at 2 per quarter to prevent the processing bottleneck seen in Q1. Allocate freed budget toward high-ROI field dinners and roundtables.
5
Build a carryover conversion dashboard for Q2 Week 1 reporting. With 334 opps and $11.8M in unconverted Meeting Booked rolling into Q2, standard quarter-start reporting will understate the true pipeline opportunity. Create a dedicated carryover view that tracks Q1 opp conversion by event, age, and stage — visible to both marketing and sales. Set a Q2 carryover target: convert 40% of the $11.8M ($4.7M) to Pre-pipeline within the first 4 weeks of Q2.
