Summary

FY27 Q1 closes this week with Events delivering $18.7M in Meeting Booked across 773 opps — a historic quarter that dwarfs every prior period combined. The final week added $338K in Meeting Booked (down from $447K prior week) as the calendar wound down. Pre-pipeline finished at $6.9M QTD, reaching 70% of the $9.84M target after a strong $628K close-week addition powered by late conversions from Digital Travel Connect ($468K) and KO Roundtable ($80K). Qualified Pipeline remains at $1.11M with no new movement in the final week. The quarter ends ~$2.9M short of the Pre-pipeline target — a significant gap, but one contextualized by the unprecedented Meeting Booked volume still working through the funnel. The key Q2 carry-in story: $11.8M in Meeting Booked has yet to reach Pre-pipeline, representing the largest conversion backlog in company history.

Key historical insights:

1. FY27 Q1 generated more Meeting Booked than the prior 4 quarters combined — by 9×. The $18.7M total was driven by a concentrated tier-1 conference calendar (ViVE, HIMSS, Shoptalk Spring, eTail PS), each individually exceeding any full-quarter total from FY26. The pipeline backlog carry-in to Q2 ($11.8M unconverted) is itself larger than any prior quarter's total Meeting Booked, meaning Q2 Pre-pipeline performance will be significantly boosted by Q1 tail conversion.

2. Conversion rate compression is the quarter's defining challenge. Pre-pipeline conversion of 37% (439 of 773 opps reaching Pre-pipeline) and Pre→Qualified at 24% both trail FY26 Q4's 38% benchmark. The sheer volume overwhelmed follow-up capacity — HIMSS (12% conversion) and KO Roundtable (15%) are the clearest examples of high-value events where processing bottlenecks, not lead quality, constrained outcomes.

3. Small-format events remain the ROI champions. Boston Yacht Dinner (8.7× ROI, $7.5K spend), McKinsey Alumni (50% win rate, $2.4K), and Ariat Nashville (40% Qual rate, $10K) delivered outsized returns. The top 5 ROI events all cost under $18K. As Q2 planning begins, a 70/30 split between tier-1 conferences and high-frequency intimate formats would balance volume generation with conversion efficiency.

Wins, Concerns & Watch Items

✦ Wins
Digital Travel Connect converted 100% of Meeting Booked to Pre-pipeline — all $468K moved through in the final week, making it the most efficient event by conversion rate among campaigns with meaningful ARR. KO Roundtable (APAC) broke through with $80K in Pre-pipeline after showing zero movement for weeks, indicating late-quarter follow-up is taking hold. ViVE closes Q1 as the top-performing tier-1 event with $935K Pre-pipeline from $3.28M Meeting Booked (29% conversion) and $310K in Qualified — the only FY27 Q1 conference to generate material Qualified pipeline. AICC (APAC) maintained its best-in-class conversion at 68% Pre-pipeline rate ($370K from $543K), validating APAC conference format effectiveness.


✦ Concerns
HIMSS closes Q1 as the portfolio's largest conversion failure. With $3.65M Meeting Booked and only $440K Pre-pipeline (12% conversion), it holds $3.21M in unconverted pipeline — the single largest carry-in balance. Only 5 of 43 opps reached Pre-pipeline, and zero reached Qualified. This demands immediate Q2 triage. Qualified Pipeline had zero movement for the second consecutive week — the $1.11M QTD total finished entirely dependent on ViVE ($310K) and CCW Orlando ($800K). The quarter closes with only 47 Qualified opps from 773 Meeting Booked (6%), well below the 15% historical benchmark. The $2.9M Pre-pipeline gap to target means the Events OKR finished at 70% attainment — while strong in absolute terms, it reflects systemic processing constraints that must be addressed in Q2 planning.


✦ Watch Items
Shoptalk Spring carries $2.87M in unconverted Meeting Booked into Q2 — with 38 of 53 opps still pre-Pre-pipeline, this is the second-largest conversion opportunity behind HIMSS. Early Q2 follow-up cadences will determine whether this pipeline materializes or ages out. Fintech Meetup improved slightly with $110K now in Pre-pipeline (from zero last week), but $924K remains unconverted with only 1 of 12 opps progressed — the 3+ week age on these opps is approaching critical decay thresholds. CCW Orlando (FY26 Q4 carryover) holds $800K in Qualified across its top opps — these must be actively managed through Q2 close processes.

Recommended Actions
1
Launch Q2 "Conversion Sprint" on the $11.8M carry-in backlog. The unconverted Meeting Booked pipeline is the largest lever for Q2 Pre-pipeline performance. Prioritize by value: HIMSS ($3.21M), Shoptalk Spring ($2.87M), and Fintech Meetup ($924K) represent $7.0M of the backlog. Assign dedicated SDR pods to each event's stalled opps in week 1 of Q2, with a target of converting 30% within 14 days. Every 10% of the $11.8M converted yields $1.18M — exceeding the average weekly Pre-pipeline run rate.
2
Conduct a full HIMSS post-mortem this week. At 12% Pre-pipeline conversion on $3.65M Meeting Booked, HIMSS significantly underperformed ViVE (29%) and eTail PS (39%) despite similar scale. Investigate whether the gap is driven by lead quality (healthcare vertical readiness), event timing (late March, limiting follow-up runway), or SDR capacity allocation. The findings should directly inform whether HIMSS returns to the FY28 calendar and at what investment level.
3
Protect the $1.11M Qualified pipeline through Q2 close. The ViVE ($310K) and CCW Orlando ($800K) Qualified balances are the only material Qualified pipeline from Events this quarter. Ensure each of the underlying opps has confirmed next steps, executive sponsorship, and close timelines documented in the first week of Q2. Any slippage risks Events' Q2 Closed Won contribution.
4
Restructure Q2 event cadence based on Q1 conversion data. The clear pattern: events held in weeks 1–8 of the quarter (ViVE, eTail PS, CCW Exchange UK) achieved 29–42% Pre-pipeline conversion, while late-quarter events (HIMSS week 8, Shoptalk week 9–10) converted at 12–28%. Front-load Q2 tier-1 events into the first 8 weeks and reserve weeks 9–13 for small-format dinners and roundtables that convert faster. Also increase the allocation to intimate formats — events under $20K spend delivered 4–9× ROI vs. 0–1.7× for large conferences.
5
Address the Qualified conversion bottleneck structurally. Only $1.11M of $6.9M Pre-pipeline converted to Qualified (16%) — down from 24% last quarter's pace. With 439 opps in Pre-pipeline and only 138 reaching Qualified across the full portfolio (31%), the Discovery-to-Qualified handoff is the funnel's tightest chokepoint. Recommend a joint Sales-Marketing review of Qualified criteria and whether current thresholds are calibrated for the higher volume reality of Q1's event portfolio.
