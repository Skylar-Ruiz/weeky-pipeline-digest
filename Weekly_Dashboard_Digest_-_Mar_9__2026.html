Six weeks in — 41% of Q1 elapsed — and the funnel story remains bifurcated. Top-of-funnel has stabilized near pace: MQLs improved to 39% projected (440 actual, 16% of target), Pre-Pipeline held at 40% projected ($8.1M actual), and MQL volume surged in Week 5 to 158 — the quarter's best week. But Discovery at 14% projected and Qualified at 15% remain stuck at roughly a third of pace. The conversion bottleneck is now the defining challenge — $19.8M in projected Pre-Pipeline sits upstream while only $5.7M projected has reached Discovery. Events are the standout engine, driving $6.7M in attributed Pre-Pipeline ARR — led by ViVE ($3.1M) and eTail Palm Springs ($1.7M). AI pipeline outpaces Comms 6.7:1.

What's working
Wins & highlights
MQL volume hit a quarter high in Week 5
158 MQLs · ▲53% WoW
The highest single-week volume this quarter, driven by Paid Search (51), Direct (27), and Organic Search (24). Total MQL pacing improved from 31% to 39% projected — closing the gap toward the 41% benchmark.
AI pipeline dominance widening — 6.7:1 ratio
$855K AI vs. $127K Comms Qualified
AI Qualified projected at 20% of target vs. Comms at just 6%. The gap widened from 5.2:1 last week — the market continues pulling hard toward the AI offering.
Discovery rebounded sharply in Week 5
$610K · ▲401% WoW
After a dismal $122K in Week 4, Discovery snapped back to $610K — the second-strongest week this quarter. Events contributed $410K, with AMER adding $460K. First signs the Pre-Pipeline surge is starting to convert.
Commercial Qualified pacing at 47%
$303K actual ($737K projected) against a $1.57M target. The only market segment pacing above the 41% benchmark — a bright spot in an otherwise challenged Qualified picture.

Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Discovery Pipeline still deeply behind at 14% projected
$2.4M actual (6%) · $5.7M projected of $40.4M target
Only 6% of the Discovery target has actually been hit. Even projected attainment ($5.7M) is barely one-third of the 41% pace benchmark. APAC Discovery at 9% projected is the weakest region. The $19.8M Pre-Pipeline reservoir is not converting fast enough.
Enterprise Qualified deteriorated to 4% projected
$174K actual (1.7%) · $423K projected of $10.2M target
Down from 5% projected last week. Enterprise represents 65% of the total Qualified target and has generated just $174K in actual pipe. This remains the single most critical gap in the entire funnel.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion stuck at 9%
9% · ↓49% QoQ
Down from 17% in FY26 Q4. The mid-funnel efficiency problem persists — every lead generated at the top converts at roughly half its expected rate. This compounds every upstream volume gain.
SALs pacing slipped from 37% to 35% projected
40 actual (14% of target) · 35% projected
Week 5 delivered only 5 Sales Accepted meetings, continuing the decline (11 → 5 → 10 → 8 → 5). Rising MQLs are not translating to meetings — the handoff gap is widening.
Inbound Paid Discovery at less than 1%
Only $77K in Discovery ARR against an $8.1M target — 0.9% pacing. The worst-performing channel by a wide margin. Needs urgent intervention to justify continued spend.
APAC Qualified collapsed to 2% projected
$53K actual, $129K projected against a $5.2M target. No new Qualified pipeline from APAC since Week 1. The region represents 33% of the Qualified target.
Social followers still lagging at 22%
654 followers against a 3,000 Q1 target. Weekly gains remain inconsistent — 187 one week, single digits the next. At current pace, projected attainment falls well short of goal.

Key takeaway: Week 5 reversed the downstream drought. Discovery surged 401% to $610K — the first meaningful conversion from the Pre-Pipeline buildup. This week
Recommended actions
1
Convert the Events Pre-Pipeline surge. $6.7M in events Pre-Pipeline ARR is the quarter's biggest asset — but only if it moves downstream. Prioritize the top 25 event opps by value (starting with ViVE's $3.1M and eTail's $1.7M), assign dedicated owners, and set a 2-week Discovery conversion SLA.
2
Enterprise pipeline intervention — executive sponsorship required. At 4% projected (1.7% actual) against 65% of the Qualified target, Enterprise is the single largest risk. Escalate to CRO for a top-10 Enterprise account blitz with multi-threaded executive engagement.
3
Diagnose the SAL bottleneck. MQLs are up 53% WoW but SALs dropped to 5. The MQL-to-SAL handoff is breaking — audit whether leads are sitting unworked, the acceptance bar has shifted, or routing is misaligned.
4
Inbound Paid emergency review. At 0.9% Discovery pacing against an $8.1M target, Paid is essentially non-contributing. Conduct an immediate audit of targeting, creative, and landing page conversion. Consider reallocating budget if fixes can't be identified within 2 weeks.
5
APAC regional escalation. Discovery at 9% projected and Qualified at 2%. No new Qualified pipeline from APAC in five weeks. Initiate a 30-day sprint with regional leadership — the back half of the quarter starts now.
