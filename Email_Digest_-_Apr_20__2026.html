Wins, Concerns & Watch Items
✓
Wins
Newsletter open rate hit a 2026 high at 44.04%, continuing a three-month recovery from February's 34.69% trough — now the highest since December 2025's Exec edition (46.12%). The April AMER newsletter specifically achieved 44.09% open rate and 4.97% CTR on 32,734 delivered, making it the strongest single high-volume newsletter send since January's AMER edition (42.22% on 37,530 delivered). The EMEA edition maintained strong parity at 43.70% open rate, narrowing the AMER-EMEA gap to just 0.4pp — the tightest in 2026. The Spark #save-your-seat CTA generated 649 combined clicks (AMER 616 + EMEA 33) from the newsletter alone, the second-highest single-link click count in 6 months. Portfolio-level CTR improved to 4.7% (1.7× benchmark), up from last week's 4.3%. Delivery infrastructure holds excellent at 99% delivered. The AICC survey sustained its extraordinary 41.22% CTR.
!
Concerns
Spark event invites continue to suppress portfolio metrics. The three Spark sends in the 30-day window (Invite 4, Invite 5, Invite 6 A/B) account for 36,353 sent emails with a blended 12.97% open rate — 48% of all volume but dragging the portfolio open rate down by an estimated 9pp. The Invite 6 A/B test is the new worst performer at 9.78% open and 1.07% bounce rate (190 bounces) — the single largest bounce source in the portfolio. Unsubscribe rate at 0.3% remains 2× above the 0.15% benchmark, unchanged for three consecutive weeks; the newsletter alone generated 122 unsubscribes (0.32%), suggesting it's not solely a Spark problem. Bounce rate ticked up from 0.5% to 0.8%, still below the 1.0% ceiling but trending in the wrong direction due to the Invite 6 A/B test's high bounce count.
△
Watch Items
Newsletter CTR continues its gradual decline: Feb 6.87% → Mar 4.78% → Apr 4.59%. While still well above the 2.78% benchmark, the trend warrants monitoring — the February peak was inflated by the Spark event CTA, but April's 4.59% is approaching the 180-day trendline floor. Spark Invite 6 A/B test results pending: At 9.78% open on 17,534 sent, this is the weakest open in the Spark series — worth analysing which subject line variant performed better and whether the audience has fully fatigued. Spark event is May 7: With 2.5 weeks until the event, expect at least one more invite push; consider whether additional volume will further erode list health. EMEA newsletter engagement at 43.70% open is excellent but CTR at 2.36% (vs. AMER's 4.97%) represents a 2.6pp gap — the lowest EMEA CTR of 2026 and worth investigating content-click parity.
Recommended Actions
1
Audit the Spark Invite 6 A/B test results immediately. At 9.78% open on 17,534 sent, this is the lowest-performing email in the entire 30-day portfolio. Determine which subject line variant won, quantify the open-rate delta between variants, and use the learnings to inform any final pre-event push. If neither variant exceeded 12%, consider a fundamentally different messaging approach for the last invite — something that breaks from the "autonomous AI agent" framing that has fatigued the list.
2
Cap remaining Spark event invites to suppress-engaged-only sends.
