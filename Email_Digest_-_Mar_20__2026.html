Friday, March 20, 2026 · FY2027 Q1 · Week 7 of 13

Summary

Email performance continued to strengthen in the week ending March 16. Open rate climbed to 37.65%, the highest of the rolling 30-day window — up +8.1pp WoW and well above the 31.35% benchmark. Click-through rate eased slightly to 4.34% (−0.71pp) but remains nearly 2× the industry benchmark of 2.78%. The March newsletter was the standout send, reaching 38,500+ contacts with a 39.9% open rate and 4.91% CTR. The one area demanding attention: unsubscribe rate sits at 0.41%, nearly 3× the 0.15% benchmark, driven primarily by the Delight Spark event invite series. Delivery rate holds strong at 97.88%.

Wins, Concerns & Watch Items
✓
Wins
Open rate at 34.73% beats the industry benchmark by 3.4pp, with this week's sends hitting 37.65% — the highest in the 30-day window. The March newsletter is the engine: 39.9% open rate on 39K sends with a 4.91% CTR, nearly double benchmark. Delivery rate holds steady at 97.88%, confirming healthy list hygiene across programs.
!
Concerns
Unsubscribe rate of 0.41% is 2.7× above the 0.15% benchmark — the clearest red flag in the dashboard. The Spark event invite series contributes disproportionately: its 14,780-person send carried a 0.40% unsub rate, and the ViVE booth email hit 2.52%. These high-frequency, broad-audience event invites are creating opt-out pressure. Bounce rate at 1.18% is also slightly above the 1% benchmark — the Spark Invite 2 email alone generated 352 bounces (2.39% rate), suggesting stale list segments.
⚠
Watch Items
Delight Spark event emails are significantly underperforming at 17.51% open rate — half the portfolio average. The third invite ("Come see how a top US retailer is reinventing the sales floor with AI") opened at just 11.57% to 13,720 recipients. This suggests audience fatigue or poor targeting. Additionally, CTR dipped 0.71pp this week despite rising opens — worth monitoring whether the open-to-click conversion continues to soften.
Recommended Actions
1
Audit and suppress the Spark invite list. The three-touch Spark series has driven 56 unsubscribes and 387 bounces on only 14,780 sends. Before any Invite 4, scrub the list of non-openers from Invite 1–3, remove bounced addresses, and apply engagement scoring to prevent sending to cold contacts.
2
Refresh creative for the Spark event series. Open rates of 11–15% suggest both subject lines and preview text need rework. Test shorter, curiosity-driven subject lines modeled on top performers ("Great meeting you!" at 43.33%). Consider switching from promotional framing to a personal invite from a named speaker.
3
Double down on newsletter content strategy. The "Software Ate the World" essay generated 726 clicks — more than all product release links combined. Pitch two more long-form thought leadership pieces for the April newsletter. The hipages customer story (334 clicks) also performed well — queue another customer proof point for the next send.
4
Investigate the open-to-click gap. Open rates rose +8.1pp this week while CTR dropped −0.71pp, meaning more people opened but fewer clicked through. Review CTA placement, button visibility, and content hierarchy in the March newsletter template — particularly whether the "Software Ate the World" essay link was prominent enough above the fold for EMEA recipients (EMEA newsletter had 3.48% CTR vs. AMER's 5.13%).
