Seven weeks in — 49% of Q1 elapsed — and the funnel is showing its first real signs of downstream momentum. SALs surged to 51% projected, jumping 16 percentage points in a single week as 14 meetings were accepted — matching the quarter's best week and finally breaking the bottleneck flagged for three consecutive reports. Pre-Pipeline accelerated to 46% projected ($11.1M actual) on a strong $2.7M Week 6, and Discovery climbed to 17% projected ($3.6M actual) after an impressive $1.3M week — the quarter's highest. But the picture remains structurally challenged: Qualified held flat at 15% projected with only $137K added in Week 6, and MQL pacing slipped a point to 38% as volume fell to 80 after the prior week's 155. EMEA burst onto the scene with $550K in Discovery — its first contribution this quarter — anchored by CCW Exchange UK. The AI-to-Comms Qualified ratio stands at 7.8:1 ($992K vs. $127K). The conversion engine is warming but the back half of Q1 needs to sustain this velocity to close the gap.

Sales Accepted meetings surged from 5 to 14, tying the quarter's best week and reversing a five-week decline. SAL pacing jumped from 35% to 51% projected — now the only metric tracking above the 49% elapsed benchmark. The MQL-to-SAL handoff that was flagged as broken appears to be responding.
Discovery hit a quarter high — $1.28M in Week 6

The highest Discovery week of Q1, surpassing even the $851K in Week 3. Thirteen opps entered Discovery — also the best weekly volume. EMEA contributed $550K (its first Discovery pipe this quarter), AMER added $425K, and APAC posted $307K. The Pre-Pipeline dam is cracking open.
EMEA ignited with $550K Discovery and $880K Pre-Pipeline

EMEA had contributed zero Discovery ARR through the first five weeks. Week 6 changed that dramatically — CCW Exchange UK ($323K Discovery) and CX Solutions UK ($70K) led the breakthrough. EMEA Pre-Pipeline now stands at $2.2M (25% projected), with Discovery at $550K. A region that was invisible is now in play.
Pre-Pipeline pacing improved to 46% — closing in on pace
$11.1M actual · 22% of $49.8M target
Up 6 percentage points from 40% last week. Week 6 delivered $2.7M in new Pre-Pipeline opps across 27 opportunities, with EMEA ($880K) and APAC ($493K) diversifying beyond the AMER-heavy mix. Pre-Pipeline is now the closest it's been to the elapsed benchmark all quarter.
Branded search volume on track to exceed target — 156% projected
3,815 actual branded impressions against a 5,000 Q1 target, with projected attainment of 7,803. Consistent weekly volume of 580–680 impressions demonstrates sustained awareness momentum.

Key takeaway: Week 6 was the strongest Discovery week of the quarter by a wide margin — $1.28M across 13 opps, more than double last week.
