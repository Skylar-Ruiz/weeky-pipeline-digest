Five weeks into Q1, the funnel picture has split in two. The top of funnel has dramatically rebounded — Pre-Pipeline surged from 4% to 40% projected pacing on the strength of a massive Events pipeline week ($3.5M). But mid-to-lower funnel remains stuck: Discovery at 14% and Qualified at 14%, both deeply behind target. The bright spots: branded search is now at 160% of target, SALs pacing improved to 37%, and AI product pipeline continues to outpace Comms. The critical gap has shifted downstream — converting the Pre-Pipeline surge into Discovery and Qualified is now the #1 priority.

What's working
Wins & highlights
Pre-Pipeline exploded — from 4% to 40% in two weeks
$19.5M of $49.8M target
Events drove the surge with $3.5M in Pre-Pipeline created, including $1.9M in the week of Feb 28 alone. AMER Pre-Pipeline pacing hit 49% — the strongest regional performance.
Branded search blowing past target
160% paced
2,752 branded impressions against a 5,000 Q1 goal. Sustained weekly volumes of 600–680 impressions since early Feb — brand awareness investment is compounding.
Sales Accepted meetings pacing ahead
37% paced · 102 of 277 target
Up from 27% two weeks ago. At 37% with 38% of the quarter elapsed, this is nearly on track — the strongest pacing metric in the funnel.
AI product pipeline continues to dominate
AI Qualified Pipeline at $655K (19% paced) vs. Comms at $127K (7% paced) — a 5.2:1 ratio. The market signal toward AI offerings remains clear and strengthening.
Sales Prospecting delivering above pace
$1.03M in Qualified from Sales Prospecting (32% paced against $3.2M target). Outbound execution remains the most consistent qualified pipeline source.
Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Discovery Pipeline severely behind at 14%
$5.5M of $38.7M target
With 38% of Q1 elapsed, Discovery is pacing at barely a third of where it needs to be. AMER Discovery is at 17%, APAC at just 10%. The Pre-Pipeline surge has not yet converted downstream.
Enterprise Qualified still in emergency territory
5% of $10.2M target
$505K in Enterprise Qualified pipe — improved from 1% but still deeply behind at 5% pacing. Enterprise represents 65% of the Qualified target and remains the largest gap in the funnel.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion still cratering
7% (↓61% QoQ)
Down from 17% in FY26 Q4 — worse than the 10% reported two weeks ago. The efficiency leak at the middle of the funnel is deepening, not improving.
Inbound Paid and Organic severely underperforming
W-shaped attribution shows Inbound Paid at $100K of $4.1M target (~2%) and Organic at $110K of $1.5M (~7%). These channels need urgent attention to diversify beyond Events dependency.
APAC Discovery stalling at 10%
Only $1.4M Discovery against a $14.2M target. APAC Qualified even worse at 3% ($154K of $5.3M). The region needs a focused acceleration plan.
Social followers lagging at 21% of goal
623 followers against a 3,000 Q1 target. Weekly gains have been inconsistent — a strong week of 187 followed by single digits. Needs a sustained organic and paid social push to close the gap.

Key takeaway: Week 4 was a tale of extremes. Pre-Pipeline exploded — up 418% to $3.7M — driven by a massive Events haul. MQLs surged 51% to 104, the highest weekly volume this quarter. But the downstream story is concerning: Discovery collapsed 91% to just $73K, and Qualified generated zero. Sales Accepted continued to slip (10 → 8). The funnel is filling at the top but not converting — handoff velocity is the critical unlock.

This week
Recommended actions
1
Activate the Pre-Pipeline-to-Discovery conversion engine. With $19.5M in Pre-Pipeline and only $5.5M in Discovery, the handoff is the bottleneck. Identify the top 20 Pre-Pipeline opps by value and fast-track them through Discovery qualification.
2
Enterprise pipeline escalation. At 5% Qualified pacing against 65% of the target, Enterprise remains critical. Expand the war room to include CS and Product for multi-threaded account engagement on the top 15 Enterprise opps.
3
Diagnose the MQL-to-Qualified freefall. Conversion dropped from 17% to 7% QoQ — now 61% worse. Audit lead scoring, qualification criteria, and the SDR-to-AE handoff to find the break point.
4
APAC regional intervention. Discovery at 10% and Qualified at 3%. Commission a 30-day sprint plan with regional leadership to address pipeline generation and conversion gaps.
5
Lean harder into AI positioning. AI pipeline outpaces Comms 5.2:1 in Qualified. Reallocate marketing spend and messaging to accelerate the product-market fit signal — especially in Paid and Organic channels that are underperforming.
