Eight weeks in — 57% of Q1 elapsed — and the funnel is showing real momentum for the first time this quarter. Opps Created surged to 58% projected ($28.7M actual against $49.8M target), up 18pp from Week 6, driven by a massive $4.9M Week 7 — the quarter's best week by far. HIMSS alone contributed $3.5M in new Pre-Pipeline ARR. Discovery jumped to 22% projected ($10.1M actual), up from 14%, with $1.3M entering in Week 7. SALs also improved sharply to 51% projected (141 actual). But Qualified remains the critical bottleneck at just 18% projected ($2.8M of $15.7M) — the $10M sitting in Discovery is not converting fast enough. EMEA is the sole geo pacing above target on Opps Created at 104% projected, while APAC remains deeply challenged at 22%. AI pipeline continues to dominate Qualified at a 10:1 ratio over Comms ($1.46M vs. $147K). Events remain the engine — $10.6M in attributed Opps Created ARR, representing 81% of all channel-attributed pipeline.

What's working
Wins & highlights
Opps Created hit a quarter-high in Week 7 — HIMSS delivers
$4.9M in Week 7 · ▲76% WoW
The largest single week of Opps Created this quarter. HIMSS Las Vegas drove $3.5M in new pipeline with 42 opps from a single event. Combined with the continued ViVE pipeline ($3.3M QTD) and eTail Palm Springs ($1.7M QTD), the events engine is firing. Opps Created pacing jumped from 40% to 58% projected.
Discovery pipeline more than doubled — $10.1M actual
22% projected · ▲+8pp WoW
Discovery surged from $2.4M two weeks ago to $10.1M — adding $1.3M in Week 7 and $750K in early Week 8. AMER leads with $3.3M in Discovery ARR. The EMEA pipeline is now appearing with $1.3M entering Discovery, anchored by CCW Exchange UK ($323K) and ViVE-sourced pipeline moving downstream.
SALs rebounded strongly — 51% projected
141 actual · ▲+16pp WoW
After weeks of concern about the MQL-to-SAL handoff, Sales Accepted meetings improved significantly. The 80 SALs in the trailing period show the bottleneck is easing. This is the first time SALs have paced above 50% this quarter.
EMEA Opps Created pacing at 104% — above target
$5.2M projected · $2.9M actual
EMEA is the only geo exceeding its Opps Created target, driven by CCW Exchange UK ($933K) and strong event follow-through. EMEA also added 6 new Discovery opps and $1.3M in Discovery pipeline — a region that was quiet earlier in the quarter is now contributing meaningfully.
Qualified pipeline added $470K in Week 7 — best week this quarter
$470K · Events-sourced from CCW Orlando
The strongest single Qualified week of Q1, driven by CCW Orlando ($470K). Mid-Market segment leads Qualified at $1.1M of the $1.6M total. Qualified pacing improved from 15% to 18% projected.
Needs attention
Concerns & problem areas
Qualified Pipeline still critically behind at 18% projected
$2.8M actual (18%) · $15.7M target
Despite improvement from 15% to 18%, Qualified pacing is still running at roughly one-third of the 57% elapsed benchmark. $10.1M sits in Discovery that hasn't converted — the Discovery-to-Qualified conversion rate needs to accelerate sharply in the final 5 weeks. At current pace, the quarter will finish at roughly $2.8M — an $12.9M shortfall.
Enterprise Qualified at 5% projected — the quarter's biggest gap
$174K actual (1.7%) · $307K projected of $10.2M target
Enterprise represents 65% of the total Qualified target yet has generated just $174K. Mid-Market ($1.1M) and Commercial ($323K) are carrying the Qualified pipeline. Without a dramatic Enterprise breakthrough in the final 5 weeks, the quarter's Qualified target is unreachable.
APAC pipeline deeply challenged across the funnel
Opps Created 22% · Discovery 13% · Qualified 7%
APAC Opps Created at $2.7M projected (22% of $12.5M target) and Qualified at just $371K projected (7% of $5.2M target). Only 4 Qualified opps this entire quarter. The region accounts for one-third of the global Qualified target and is tracking to miss by over $4.8M.
MQL-to-Qualified conversion worsened to 8%
8% · ↓56% QoQ
Down from 18% in FY26 Q4 and 9% two weeks ago. The mid-funnel conversion crisis continues to compound — 1,055 MQLs have generated only 13 Qualified opps this quarter. Every upstream gain is being diluted by this conversion gap.
MQL pacing slipped to 38% projected despite volume
1,055 actual · 38% projected of 2,770 target
MQL pacing actually declined 1pp from 39% despite 598 total MQLs (Leads + Contacts). Week 7 delivered 82 MQLs — a decent week but not enough to close the gap. Paid Search (132 QTD) and Direct (85 QTD) remain the top channels, but the trajectory is falling short.
Inbound Paid Discovery still lagging at 3% projected
$171K in Discovery ARR against a $9.6M channel target — 2% actual. Improved from near-zero but still the weakest performing channel relative to target. Paid Search is generating MQLs (132 QTD — the #1 channel) but the downstream conversion is severely broken.
HIMSS pipeline conversion is the key risk
HIMSS generated $3.5M in Opps Created with 42 opps — but $0 has reached Discovery so far. This is the quarter's single largest pipeline source and needs to start converting immediately to impact Q1 Qualified numbers. Pre-Pipeline-to-Discovery conversion for HIMSS is 0% vs. 13% for ViVE and 19% for eTail Palm Springs.
Social followers at 23% — still significantly behind
700 followers against a 3,000 Q1 target. Weekly gains remain low (22 last week, 16 the week before). At current pace, projected attainment of ~1,235 falls well short of the 3,000 goal.
