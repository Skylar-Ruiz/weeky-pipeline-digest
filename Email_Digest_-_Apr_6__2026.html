Summary

The 30-day email portfolio continues to outperform industry benchmarks on every engagement metric except unsubscribes. Open rate stands at 32.8%, above the 31.35% benchmark, and click-through rate of 5.1% runs nearly 2× the industry standard of 2.78%. Delivered rate holds at 99% with bounce rate a healthy 0.6% — well below the 1.0% ceiling. The March newsletter was the standout program, reaching 38,472 delivered contacts with a 40.3% open rate and 4.78% CTR. The AICC conference follow-up survey achieved an extraordinary 40.8% CTR on 302 delivered, proving highly targeted post-event emails can generate outsized engagement. The AI Readiness Webinar program emerged as a reliable second engine with 38.2% open rate and 4.9% CTR across 8,192 delivered. The persistent concern: unsubscribe rate at 0.32% is 2× the 0.15% benchmark, with the Delight Spark event series accounting for the bulk of bounces and opt-outs. The week ending Mar 30 saw a 75.0% open rate and 33.3% CTR on low-volume sends, while high-volume weeks consistently delivered CTR in the 3.6–5.7% range.

Wins, Concerns & Watch Items
✓
Wins
Newsletter open rate hit a 3-month high at 40.27% after February's dip to 34.67%, surpassing even January's 39.02% on 38,472 delivered contacts. CTR of 4.78% remains nearly 2× the industry benchmark. The AI Readiness Webinar program proved to be a reliable second engine: 38.15% open rate and 4.93% CTR across 8,192 delivered, demonstrating that educational content resonates deeply with this audience. The AICC conference follow-up survey achieved a remarkable 40.77% CTR — proof that hyper-targeted, timely post-event emails with a clear single CTA can generate outsized engagement. Delivery infrastructure continues to excel: 99% delivered rate and 0.6% bounce rate, both well within healthy thresholds. Conference booth follow-ups across Shoptalk (43.75%), HIMSS (45.26%), and AICC (43.05%) confirm that in-person touchpoints convert exceptionally well to email engagement.
!
Concerns
Unsubscribe rate of 0.32% remains 2× above the 0.15% benchmark. The Spark event invite series continues to be the primary driver: its 18,989-person send carried 47 total unsubscribes and 215 bounces (1.13% bounce rate) — the only major program exceeding the 1% bounce ceiling. This suggests both stale contacts and audience fatigue in the event database. The Spark Invite 4 open rate of 12.77% to 18,348 delivered is the lowest-performing email in the entire portfolio by a wide margin — the previous invite (Invite 3) performed similarly at 11.73%, confirming list exhaustion. Additionally, the Shoptalk booth follow-up shows a disconnect: a healthy 43.75% open rate but only 2.14% CTR on 320 delivered, suggesting the email content or CTA wasn't compelling enough to convert engaged openers.
⚠
Watch Items
Newsletter CTR is trending down: Jan 5.27% → Feb 6.87% → Mar 4.78%. While still well above benchmark, March's drop reflects a shift in content mix — the February issue benefited from the Spark event CTA which drove 899 clicks alone. Without a comparable conversion-focused CTA, March relied on thought leadership and customer stories, which drive awareness but fewer clicks per recipient. The AMER vs. EMEA CTR gap persists: AMER achieved 5.01% CTR vs. EMEA's 3.48% — a 1.53pp gap despite nearly identical open rates (40.30% vs. 40.07%). This suggests CTA placement or content relevance needs regional tuning. List size is contracting: AMER newsletter sends dropped from 38,089 (Jan) to 32,872 (Mar), a −13.7% decline over three months that warrants investigation into list growth and suppression policies.
